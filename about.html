About Me

Hello there! I'm Chandani Shrestha.

Dr. Chandani Shrestha is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at James Madison University. She holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Virginia Tech University, where her research focused on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). Dr. Shrestha's work integrates interdisciplinary approaches to address both societal and pedagogical challenges through the use of computing and technology. Central to her research is the exploration of technological interventions that shift power dynamics to empower underserved communitites. Dr. Shrestha has contributed to various research projects, including initiatives aimed at identifying and addressing the needs of survivors of sex trafficking, as well as broader issues concerning the condition of women in Nepal. Her recent and current primary research, ThoughSwap, is a technological system designed to enhance classroom pedagogy by transforming the infrastructure for discourse in classrooms. In addition to her research, she is deeply committed to teaching and improving pedagogical practices in higher education. Dr.Shrestha earned her Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Benedict College, SC.
